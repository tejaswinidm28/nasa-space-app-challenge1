Tracking Phytoplankton Blooms from Space

PACE's Ocean Color Instrument (OCI) allows scientists to monitor phytoplankton populations from orbit, providing crucial insights into ocean health and the marine food web. Learn how these microscopic plants play a vital role in the Earth's climate and ecosystem.

Unraveling the Mysteries of Aerosols and Clouds

PACE's polarimeters, SPEXone and HARP2, study how aerosols and clouds interact with sunlight. This data helps us understand how these atmospheric components influence the climate, ocean ecosystems, and air quality.

Benefits of PACE Data

Discover how PACE data is being used to address critical challenges:

  • **Assessing Ocean Health:** Monitor phytoplankton health, diversity, and productivity to understand the overall state of the marine ecosystem.

  • **Protecting Coastal Communities:** Track and predict harmful algal blooms that can impact human health, fisheries, and tourism.

  • **Mitigating Climate Change:** Understand the ocean's role in the carbon cycle and how climate change is affecting marine ecosystems.

  • **Managing Marine Resources:** Provide data for sustainable fisheries management and marine conservation efforts.
